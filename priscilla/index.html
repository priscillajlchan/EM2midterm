A Brief history of Vancouver’s Popular Stanley Park Seawall

Located near Downtown, Vancouver. The seawall is a scenic 22 KM path that surrounds Stanley Park. Extremely popular among locals and tourist. It’s the perfect place for a walk, cycle or jog.

Construction of the Seawall began in 1917. James Cunningham was very invovled in the building of the seawall. He surprivised the project until his last breathe. It’s unfortunte that he never got to see the seawall complete. In honors of his work, there’s a plaque dedicated to him that can be found near Siwash Rock. (the place where his ashes were scattered).

Today, the seawall still faces conflict when it comes to sharing the path with cylists and pedestrians. Collisons were becoming an issue. Currently, their still working towards expanding and extending the seawall overall. Mainly focusing along the seaside. Hopefully for year’s to come, it’ll continue to remain a favorite.
